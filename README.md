# 💧 From Drilling to Distribution  
## Understanding How a Complete Borehole Water System Works

When most people think of a **borehole**, they picture a simple hole drilled into the ground that magically produces water. The reality is very different. A reliable **borehole water system** is a carefully designed setup where multiple components work together to deliver clean, pressurised water to your property.

Across South Africa, many homeowners, farms, and businesses are investing in boreholes as municipal water becomes more expensive and unreliable. But a borehole only works properly when every part of the system — from surveying to pumps to purification — is correctly designed.

At **LiquaFlo Borehole Solutions**, we help clients across Gauteng build complete water systems that are reliable, efficient, and built for long-term performance.

---

## 🧠 Step 1: Geo Surveying – Finding the Underground Water

Before any **borehole drilling** begins, the most important step is identifying where underground water is most likely to be found.

Geo surveying and site assessments help determine the best drilling location by analysing underground formations and water potential. Drilling without a proper survey can result in poor water yield or unnecessary drilling costs.

A proper survey ensures the drilling team targets the most promising location from the start.

---

## 🛠 Step 2: Borehole Drilling – Reaching the Aquifer

Once the location has been confirmed, the drilling process begins.

A specialised **borehole drilling machine** drills through layers of soil and rock until it reaches a water-bearing aquifer. The depth varies depending on the geology of the area.

Professional drilling contractors follow strict standards such as **SABS SANS 10299-:2003** to ensure the borehole is structurally secure and protected from contamination.

After drilling is complete, the borehole is fitted with casing to prevent collapse and keep the water source protected.

---

## 🚿 Step 3: Flushing and Borehole Development

Once drilling is finished, the borehole must be cleaned and developed.

Flushing removes sand, debris, and loose particles left behind during drilling. This step improves water flow and protects the **borehole pumps** that will later be installed.

Skipping this stage can damage pumps and reduce the overall efficiency of the system.

---

## ⚙ Step 4: Pump Installation – Bringing Water to the Surface

The pump is the heart of the borehole system.

A **water borehole pump**, usually a submersible pump, is installed deep inside the borehole. Its job is to push water up through piping and underwater cabling to the surface.

Choosing the correct pump is essential. A pump that is too small may struggle to meet demand, while an oversized pump may waste energy and wear out faster.

LiquaFlo installs **SABS-approved borehole pumps** that are matched to each property's water needs.

---

## 🛢 Step 5: Water Storage and Pressure Control

Once the water reaches the surface, it needs to be stored and properly distributed.

Water tanks store the extracted water, while pressure pumps and automatic controllers regulate the flow throughout the property.

This ensures steady water pressure for:

✔ Homes  
✔ Farms and irrigation systems  
✔ Commercial facilities  
✔ Agricultural operations  

The goal is to create a smooth and reliable water supply similar to municipal systems — except powered by your own borehole.

---

## 💧 Step 6: Filtering Borehole Water

Not all underground water is immediately suitable for drinking or household use.

Depending on the geology of the area, **filtering borehole water** may require sediment filters, carbon filtration, UV sterilisation, or multi-stage purification systems.

LiquaFlo designs **tailor-made purification systems** to ensure borehole water is clean, safe, and suitable for everyday use.

---

## 🚰 Step 7: Water Distribution Across the Property

Once purification and pressure systems are installed, the water is connected to the property's plumbing or irrigation network.

From that point on, the borehole system provides a consistent supply of water for:

✔ Household use  
✔ Irrigation and landscaping  
✔ Agricultural production  
✔ Commercial facilities  

When designed correctly, the entire system operates seamlessly — delivering reliable water directly from underground to your tap.
